Voyager-1

Voyager 1 visited Jupiter in March of 1979. This was the first time a spacecraft had visited the outer solar system, and it was a major milestone in the exploration of our solar system.
Two Voyagers left Earth in 1977 on a mission to track an unusual planetary alignment that occurs every 175 years. Voyager 1 made its first visit to Jupiter in 1979, following in the footsteps of Pioneer spacecraft. The spacecraft captured the first images of the moons of Amalthea, Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. In the 2030s, the Clipper mission will fly around the icy moon to determine if it is a suitable location for microbial life. The European Space Agency intends to launch its Jupiter ICy moons Explorer (JUICE) mission from Earth in 2022.
